Engagement and Self-management System (ESMS) ()
Definition: The use or consumption of feature provided by a patient engagement self-management system (ESMS). An ESMS is a Service/System enabling the management of the patient journey inside health facilities taking into account the specific condition of the patients (e.g., disabilities). Source: Techopedia Source reference: https://www.techopedia.com/definition/24745/engagement-and-self-management-system-esms Additional information: The ESMS is a comprehensive tool that combines the features of project management, team collaboration, and user engagement software. It allows users to set goals, assign tasks, track progress, and provide feedback. The system also includes features for communication and collaboration, such as chat and video conferencing. It is designed to promote self-management by providing users with the tools they need to manage their tasks and responsibilities effectively. The system can be customized to meet the specific needs of different organizations and sectors. Example: Business Application In a business setting, the ESMS can be used to manage projects, assign tasks to team members, and track progress. The system can also be used to facilitate communication and collaboration among team members, and to provide feedback and recognition. Educational Application In an educational setting, the ESMS can be used to manage assignments, track student progress, and facilitate communication and collaboration among students and teachers. The system can also be used to provide feedback and recognition, and to promote self-management skills among students.
