Health Legislation on Governance Interoperability (ENBI) ()
Definition: Health Legislation on Governance Interoperability (ENBI) ABB is a grouping that covers the elements related to the legal and governance aspects of health interoperability. It includes elements that are crucial for the effective exchange, integration, sharing, and retrieval of electronic health information that are necessary for delivering health care, conducting health research, and managing health systems. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The ENBI ABB catalogue includes elements that are critical for ensuring the smooth interoperability of health systems. These elements are designed to address the legal and governance challenges that can arise when different health systems interact with each other. The catalogue includes elements like the 'Education Health and Care (EHC) Plan', which is a legal document that describes the education, health and social care needs of a child or young person, and the support that will be provided to meet those needs. Example: Education Health and Care (EHC) Plan The Education Health and Care (EHC) Plan is an example of an element in the ENBI ABB catalogue. This plan is a legal document that outlines the education, health and social care needs of a child or young person, and the support that will be provided to meet those needs. It is a crucial element in the interoperability of health and education systems, as it ensures that all relevant parties have access to the same information and can work together effectively to support the child or young person.
