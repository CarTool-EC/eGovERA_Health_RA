Health Legislation on Behavioural Interoperability (NNBI) ()
Definition: Health Legislation on Behavioural Interoperability (NNBI) is a grouping that covers the legal and regulatory aspects of health information exchange, focusing on the behavioural aspects of different health systems and how they interact. It includes laws, regulations, and policies that govern the exchange of health information between different health systems, with a focus on ensuring that these systems can work together effectively and efficiently. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The NNBI catalogue is an important tool for understanding and navigating the complex landscape of health legislation related to behavioural interoperability. This includes not only the technical aspects of information exchange, but also the behavioural aspects, such as how different health systems and providers interact with each other and with patients. The catalogue includes a wide range of elements, from laws and regulations to policies and guidelines, all aimed at promoting effective and efficient health information exchange. It is a valuable resource for anyone involved in health information exchange, from policymakers and regulators to health care providers and IT professionals. Example: Examples of application of the NNBI catalogue could include a health system looking to understand the legal requirements for sharing patient data with another system, a regulator seeking to develop new policies to promote interoperability, or a health IT professional trying to navigate the complex landscape of health information exchange legislation.
