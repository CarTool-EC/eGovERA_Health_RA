Hospital Blood Bank System ()
Definition: The use or consumption of features provided by a hospital blood bank system enabling the management and control of blood reserves. Source: Healthcare Information and Management Systems Society (HIMSS) Source reference: https://www.himss.org/library/hospital-blood-bank-system Additional information: The Hospital Blood Bank System is a critical component of healthcare infrastructure, ensuring the efficient management of blood resources. The system is designed to streamline the process of blood donation and distribution, ensuring that blood is available when and where it's needed. It includes features for tracking donor information, blood types, and quantities of blood and blood products. It also helps in maintaining records of blood tests, results, and transfusion reactions. The system ensures compliance with regulatory standards and helps in reducing wastage of blood. It also provides real-time inventory management, ensuring that hospitals have an adequate supply of each blood type and component. Example: Examples of application include: 1) A hospital uses the system to manage its blood supply, ensuring that it has enough blood of each type for surgeries and emergencies. 2) The system is used to track the results of blood tests, ensuring that only safe, high-quality blood is used for transfusions. 3) The system helps in managing donor information, making it easy to contact donors when their blood type is needed. 4) The system is used to maintain records of transfusion reactions, helping to improve patient safety.
