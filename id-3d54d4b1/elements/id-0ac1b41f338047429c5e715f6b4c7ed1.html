Health Legislation on Structural Interoperability (ENBI) ()
Definition: Health Legislation on Structural Interoperability (ENBI) ABB is a grouping that covers the legislative elements related to the structural interoperability of health systems, particularly focusing on electronic health record systems. It includes key legislative documents such as the Commission Recommendation 2008/594/EC of 2 July 2008. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Legislation on Structural Interoperability (ENBI) ABB is a catalogue of legislative elements that are crucial for understanding and implementing the structural interoperability of health systems. This includes, but is not limited to, the Commission Recommendation 2008/594/EC of 2 July 2008, which provides recommendations on cross-border interoperability of electronic health record systems. The purpose of these elements is to ensure that health systems can effectively communicate and exchange information, improving the quality and efficiency of healthcare services. Example: An example of an element contained in the Health Legislation on Structural Interoperability (ENBI) ABB is the 'Commission Recommendation 2008/594/EC of 2 July 2008'. This recommendation provides guidelines on how to achieve cross-border interoperability of electronic health record systems, which is a key aspect of structural interoperability in health systems.
