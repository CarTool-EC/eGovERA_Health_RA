Health Semantic Agreement Solutions ()
Definition: Health Semantic Agreement Solutions is a grouping that covers a range of elements aimed at facilitating the systematic analysis, management, and interoperability of health data across different platforms. These elements include the OMOP Common Data Model, FAIR Guiding Principles for scientific data management and stewardship, and the eHealth Digital Service Infrastructure (eHDSI) semantic framework. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The elements within the Health Semantic Agreement Solutions are designed to enhance the accessibility, interoperability, and reusability of health data. The OMOP Common Data Model allows for the systematic analysis of disparate observational databases, providing a standardized approach to data analysis. The FAIR Guiding Principles for scientific data management and stewardship offer standards and guidelines for data management, promoting the capacity of computational systems to find, access, interoperate, and reuse data with minimal human intervention. The eHealth Digital Service Infrastructure (eHDSI) semantic framework is a service for cross-border health data exchange, enabling healthcare semantic experts to co-create and evolve crucial assets that form the foundational elements of semantic interoperability for cross-border eHealth services. Example: For instance, a health research institute could use the OMOP Common Data Model to analyze data from different observational databases in a standardized manner. The FAIR Guiding Principles could be applied in the management of the institute's data, ensuring that it is findable, accessible, interoperable, and reusable. The eHDSI semantic framework could be used to facilitate the cross-border exchange of the institute's health data, allowing for collaboration with other institutes and the co-creation of new health solutions.
