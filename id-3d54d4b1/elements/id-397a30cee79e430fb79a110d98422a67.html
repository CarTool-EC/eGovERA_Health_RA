Health Ontology Solutions ()
Definition: Health Ontology Solutions ABB is a Grouping that covers a range of ontologies and classification systems used in the medical and health sector. These ontologies and systems are used to classify, monitor, and analyze various aspects of health, including diseases, medical observations, drug ingredients, and surgical procedures. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Ontology Solutions ABB includes the International Classification of Diseases for Oncology (ICD-O), which is a domain-specific extension of the International Statistical Classification of Diseases and Related Health Problems for tumour diseases. It also includes the Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes (LOINC), a universal standard for identifying medical laboratory observations. The International Classification of Diseases (ICD) is also part of this grouping, which is a diagnostic tool used to classify and monitor causes of injury and death. The Anatomical Therapeutic Chemical Classification Ontology (ATCCO) is used for the classification of active ingredients of drugs according to the organ or system on which they act and their therapeutic, pharmacological and chemical properties. Lastly, the International surgery procedure ontology establishes a framework for joint development of ontologies in the field of surgery. Example: For example, the International Classification of Diseases for Oncology (ICD-O) is widely used by cancer registries. The Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes (LOINC) is used in medical laboratories to standardize the identification of medical observations. The International Classification of Diseases (ICD) is used in health analyses, such as the study of mortality and morbidity trends. The Anatomical Therapeutic Chemical Classification Ontology (ATCCO) is used in the pharmaceutical industry for drug classification. The International surgery procedure ontology is used in the development of new ontologies in the field of surgery.
