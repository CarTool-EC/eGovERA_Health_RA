Health Digital Public Service Consumers Catalogue ()
Definition: Health Digital Public Service Consumers Catalogue is a grouping that covers a wide range of entities that act as consumers of digital public services in the health sector. These entities include hospitals, national institutes for health and welfare, patients, private healthcare institutions, health insurance companies, health professionals associations, health professionals, Ministry of Health, social insurance institutions, healthcare institutions, researchers, pharmacies, testing and experimentation facilities for health, national statistics institutes, national pension institutions, and BioBank Institutions. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: Each entity in the catalogue acts as a consumer of digital public services in the health sector, meaning they utilize digital platforms and services provided by the public sector for various health-related purposes. For instance, a hospital might use digital services for patient registration and record-keeping, while a health insurance company might use them for policy management and claims processing. The catalogue is comprehensive, covering a wide range of entities from individual health professionals and patients to large institutions and government bodies. Example: For example, a 'Patient' in this catalogue refers to a healthcare patient when she/he acts as a digital service consumer. A patient is a person receiving or registered to receive medical treatment. They might use digital public services to book appointments, access their medical records, or communicate with healthcare providers. Similarly, a 'Health insurance company' refers to health insurance companies when they act as digital public service consumers. They are financial institutions providing a range of insurance policies to protect individuals and businesses against health risk. They might use digital public services for policy management, claims processing, and other administrative tasks.
