Teaching File and Clinical Trial Export system (TCE) ()
Definition: The use or consumption of feature provided by a Teaching File and Clinical Trial Export (TCE) system. A TCE system lets users flag images and related information for automatic routing to teaching file authoring or clinical trials management systems. Source: Radiological Society of North America Source reference: https://www.rsna.org/en/research/clinical-trials-processor Additional information: The TCE system is designed to streamline the process of collecting and preparing medical imaging data for use in clinical trials and teaching files. It provides a secure and efficient method for extracting relevant images from PACS, removing any identifying patient information to ensure privacy, and exporting the anonymized data in a format suitable for further analysis or presentation. The system supports a wide range of imaging modalities and data formats, and includes features for quality control and audit trails. It is intended to improve the quality and consistency of data used in clinical trials and teaching, and to reduce the time and effort required to prepare this data. Example: Examples of application include: 1) A clinical trial investigating the effectiveness of a new cancer treatment could use the TCE system to extract and anonymize PET/CT scans from participating patients, ensuring consistent and unbiased data for analysis. 2) A medical school could use the TCE system to prepare a teaching file of anonymized MRI scans demonstrating various stages of multiple sclerosis, providing a valuable educational resource for students.
