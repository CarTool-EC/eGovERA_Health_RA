Health Digital Business Capabilities Map ()
Definition: Health Digital Business Capabilities Map ABB is a Grouping that covers a wide range of digital capabilities in the healthcare sector. These capabilities range from supporting medical consultations, crisis and threat management, logistics and distribution of medical devices, to supporting medical diagnosis and tele-diagnosis. The map also includes capabilities for access control, laboratory tests, health system governance, resource allocation, dependency care, research coordination, prescription and dispensation, patient self-care, surgery and hospitalisation, medical imaging, treatment payment management, population investigation and diagnosis, legal and quality control of medical devices, skills management, electronic health records management, health service and care monitoring, health policy making, communication and prevention, health facility and resource management, secondary use of healthcare data, integrated healthcare, workforce management, regulation of medical devices and equipment, provision of specialist and nursing cares, outcome-based care financing, and monitoring and evaluation. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Digital Business Capabilities Map ABB is a comprehensive catalogue of digital capabilities in the healthcare sector. It is designed to provide a clear and structured overview of the various digital capabilities that are essential in the modern healthcare industry. These capabilities are crucial in improving the efficiency, effectiveness, and quality of healthcare services. They enable healthcare providers to leverage digital technologies to enhance their services, improve patient outcomes, and streamline their operations. Example: For instance, the 'Consultation' capability refers to the ability to digitally support a medical consultation, including processes and activities related to digital consultations directly asked/booked by people to a healthcare provider. Another example is the 'Crisis and threat management' capability, which supports the rapid response and effective information communication to the public and to health professionals during a health crisis or a long-term threat.
