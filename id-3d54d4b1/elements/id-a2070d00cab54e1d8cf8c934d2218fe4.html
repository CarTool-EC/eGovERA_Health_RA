Health Digital Public Services Catalogue ()
Definition: Health Digital Public Services Catalogue ABB is a grouping that covers a wide range of digital services related to healthcare. These services encompass electronic healthcare records, ePrescription, health remote sensing and continuous monitoring, eDispensation, tele-diagnosis, and many more. The purpose of these elements is to facilitate the provision of healthcare services through digital means, thereby improving the efficiency, accessibility, and quality of healthcare. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Digital Public Services Catalogue ABB includes services that enable the recording, management, and exchange of medical information, such as electronic healthcare records and ePrescriptions. It also includes services that facilitate remote healthcare, such as tele-diagnosis and tele-consultation. Additionally, it includes services that support the management of healthcare resources, such as health sector resource allocation and healthcare enterprise resource management. Furthermore, it includes services that support the management of healthcare research and development, such as EU research programmes application and health-related research programmes. Lastly, it includes services that support the management of healthcare regulation, such as health regulation management and outcome-based rates/remuneration settings. Example: An example of an element in the Health Digital Public Services Catalogue ABB is 'Electronic Healthcare Records'. This service enables the recording of medical events related to a patient throughout their entire patient journey. It contains general information, such as treatments and medical history of a patient, and is the digital equivalent of paper records or charts at a clinician's office.
