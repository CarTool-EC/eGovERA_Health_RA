Health Base Registry Catalogue ()
Definition: Health Base Registry Catalogue ABB is a Grouping that covers a comprehensive collection of registries and databases related to healthcare. It includes registries for drug interactions, authorised treatments, medical images, medical equipment, medical devices, healthcare professional qualifications, diagnoses, patient admissions, discharges and transfers, vaccines, eligible patients, hospital tickets, prescriptions, laboratory data, treatments, vaccinations, patient data, healthcare payments, healthcare facilities, dispensation reports, laboratory reports, medical images, patient summaries, and healthcare bills. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Base Registry Catalogue ABB is a comprehensive resource for healthcare professionals and administrators. It provides a centralized location for a wide range of healthcare-related data, including information on drug interactions, authorised treatments and medical equipment, medical images, healthcare professional qualifications, and more. This catalogue is essential for maintaining regulatory compliance, ensuring patient safety, and facilitating effective healthcare delivery. Each registry within the catalogue serves a specific purpose and contains detailed, up-to-date information. Example: For example, the 'Drugs interactions registry' within the Health Base Registry Catalogue ABB contains information on all available drug interactions. This can be used by healthcare professionals to ensure patient safety when prescribing medications. Similarly, the 'Authorised treatments registry' provides a list of all legally authorised treatments, helping healthcare providers to stay within regulatory guidelines. The 'Healthcare facilities registry' contains data on all healthcare facilities, which can be used for administrative and planning purposes.
