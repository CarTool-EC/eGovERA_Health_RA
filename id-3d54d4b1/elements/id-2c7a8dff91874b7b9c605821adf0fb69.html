Health Patient Summary Enablers ()
Definition: Health Patient Summary Enablers (ABB) is a grouping that covers a range of application services designed to facilitate the management and delivery of patient care. These services include systems for admission, discharge and transfer, identification and registration, engagement and self-management, electronic health records, patient summary, patient management information, clinical information, social service and patient relations, chronic care management, medical devices, implants registry, diagnosis, health remote sensing and continuous monitoring, and telemetry monitoring. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Patient Summary Enablers (ABB) is a comprehensive catalogue of application services that are integral to the healthcare industry. These services are designed to streamline various aspects of patient care, from admission to discharge, and include systems for managing electronic health records, patient summaries, and clinical information. They also encompass services for patient engagement and self-management, social service and patient relations, chronic care management, and patient identification and registration. Additionally, they include systems for managing medical devices, implants registry, and diagnosis, as well as services for health remote sensing and continuous monitoring, and telemetry monitoring. Example: For instance, the Admission, Discharge and Transfer System (ADTS) is used to manage the process of admitting, discharging and transferring patients within a healthcare facility. The Electronic Health Records System (EHRS) is used to digitally store and manage patients' health information. The Patient Management Information System is used to manage patient data and streamline administrative tasks, while the Clinical Information System (CIS) is used to manage clinical information and patient care data. The Telemetry Monitoring System (TMS) is used for remote monitoring of patients' health status.
