Health Controlled Vocabulary Solutions ()
Definition: Health Controlled Vocabulary Solutions ABB is a Grouping that covers a range of elements that provide structured, standardized, and internationally recognized terminologies, classifications, and ontologies in the field of health and medicine. These elements are designed to facilitate the joint development of ontologies, labeling and storing of patient information in electronic medical record systems, defining health terminologies for consumers, describing the process of primary care, and identifying medical laboratory observations. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Controlled Vocabulary Solutions ABB includes elements such as the International Classification of Diseases for Oncology, which establishes a framework for joint development of ontologies in the field of Disease; the Systematised Nomenclature of Medicine (SNOMED), a large structured dictionary used in electronic medical record systems for labeling patient information; the International consumer health terminology, an international dictionary about Health terminology; the International Clinical Terminology for Primary Care, a set of definitions of terms that describe the process of primary care; and the Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes (LOINC), a database and universal standard for identifying medical laboratory observations. Example: For instance, the International Classification of Diseases for Oncology can be used by oncologists to classify and code different types of cancer, while the Systematised Nomenclature of Medicine (SNOMED) can be used by healthcare providers to label and store patient information in electronic medical record systems. The International consumer health terminology can be used by consumers to understand health-related terminologies, while the International Clinical Terminology for Primary Care can be used by primary care providers to describe their care process. Lastly, the Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes (LOINC) can be used by medical laboratories to identify and code their observations.
