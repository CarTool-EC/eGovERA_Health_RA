Health Controlled Vocabulary Catalogue ()
Definition: Health Controlled Vocabulary Catalogue ABB is a grouping that covers the elements related to health-specific terminologies and taxonomies, particularly focusing on national specific taxonomies. These taxonomies consist of term names and labels that are specific to public service information in the health sector. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Controlled Vocabulary Catalogue ABB is a comprehensive collection of health-related terminologies and taxonomies. It is specifically designed to include national specific taxonomies, which are sets of term names and labels that are unique and specific to public service information in the health sector. This catalogue is instrumental in standardizing health-related terminologies, ensuring consistency and accuracy in their usage across different platforms and services. It is a crucial resource for health professionals, researchers, policy makers, and anyone involved in the health sector who needs to use or understand health-specific terminologies. Example: For instance, a health professional in the United States might refer to the 'Affordable Care Act' while a health professional in the United Kingdom might use the term 'National Health Service'. These are examples of national specific taxonomies that would be included in the Health Controlled Vocabulary Catalogue ABB.
