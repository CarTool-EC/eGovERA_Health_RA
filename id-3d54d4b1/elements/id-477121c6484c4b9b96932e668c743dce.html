Health Hospital Enterprise Resource Management Technical Interoperability Agreement Solutions ()
Definition: Health Hospital Enterprise Resource Management Technical Interoperability Agreement Solutions (HHERMTIAS) is a grouping that covers a range of technical solutions designed to facilitate interoperability in the context of health hospital enterprise resource management. These solutions are based on specific agreements or contracts that define the standards and protocols for sharing and exchanging data and information, with the aim of improving the efficiency, effectiveness, and quality of healthcare services. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The HHERMTIAS catalogue includes elements such as 'IHE SVS: Sharing Value Sets', which refers to a contract for sharing value sets in the context of Integrating the Healthcare Enterprise (IHE) Shared Value Sets (SVS). Value sets are lists of codes and corresponding terms, from health terminology standards, that define clinical concepts to support effective clinical communication. This element represents a technical solution for sharing these value sets across different healthcare information systems, to ensure consistent use and interpretation of clinical data, and thereby enhance the interoperability of these systems. Example: An example of application of the 'IHE SVS: Sharing Value Sets' solution could be a hospital implementing this solution to enable its different information systems (e.g., electronic health record system, laboratory information system, radiology information system) to consistently use and interpret the same value sets for clinical data, such as diagnosis codes, procedure codes, and medication codes. This would facilitate accurate and efficient exchange and use of clinical data across these systems, improving the quality of patient care and hospital operations.
