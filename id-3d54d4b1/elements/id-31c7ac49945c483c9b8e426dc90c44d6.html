Health Data Exchange Technical Agreement Solutions ()
Definition: Health Data Exchange Technical Agreement Solutions ABB is a Grouping that covers a range of technical solutions and contracts designed to facilitate the exchange of health data across different platforms and organizations. These solutions are based on international standards and protocols, ensuring interoperability and secure data sharing in the healthcare sector. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Data Exchange Technical Agreement Solutions ABB includes elements like 'IHE XD-SD: Cross-Enterprise Sharing of Scanned Documents', 'IHE XDS.b: Cross-Enterprise Document Sharing', 'IHE XDM: Cross-Enterprise Document Media Interchange', and 'Health Level 7 International - HL7'. These elements represent different technical solutions and contracts that enable the sharing of health data across different enterprises and media. They are based on the standards set by Integrating the Healthcare Enterprise (IHE) and Health Level Seven International (HL7), two leading organizations in health data standards. Example: For instance, 'IHE XD-SD: Cross-Enterprise Sharing of Scanned Documents' is a solution that enables the sharing of scanned documents across different healthcare enterprises. Similarly, 'IHE XDS.b: Cross-Enterprise Document Sharing' is a contract that facilitates the sharing of health documents across different enterprises. 'IHE XDM: Cross-Enterprise Document Media Interchange' is a solution that enables the interchange of health documents across different media. Lastly, 'Health Level 7 International - HL7' is a contract based on the HL7 standards, which are widely used in the healthcare sector for data exchange, integration, sharing, and retrieval.
