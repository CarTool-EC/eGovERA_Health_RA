Health Semantic Agreement Catalogue ()
Definition: Health Semantic Agreement Catalogue ABB is a grouping that covers the semantic agreements between public administrations (national and European) that define the methods and format for sharing medical images. This catalogue is primarily focused on the imaging exchange format, which is a critical component in the healthcare sector for the exchange of medical images. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Semantic Agreement Catalogue ABB is a crucial tool in the healthcare sector, particularly in the field of medical imaging. It contains the semantic agreements between public administrations, both at the national and European level, that outline the methods and format for sharing medical images. These agreements are essential for ensuring the seamless and efficient exchange of medical images between different healthcare providers and institutions. They also help to maintain the integrity and quality of the images, which is vital for accurate diagnosis and treatment. The catalogue is a valuable resource for healthcare providers, administrators, and policymakers involved in the management and exchange of medical images. Example: For instance, a hospital in France may need to share a patient's MRI scans with a specialist in Germany. The Health Semantic Agreement Catalogue ABB provides the necessary semantic agreement that outlines the methods and format for sharing these images. This ensures that the images are shared efficiently and accurately, and that they can be easily interpreted by the specialist in Germany.
