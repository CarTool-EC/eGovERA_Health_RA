Patient Management Information System ()
Definition: The use or consumption of feature provided by a patient management information system (PMIS). A PMIS is a comprehensive, integrated information systems designed to manage the information of patients of a hospital and manage the service processes. Source: Healthcare Information and Management Systems Society (HIMSS) Source reference: https://www.himss.org/library/healthcare-information-systems Additional information: The Patient Management Information System is a comprehensive system that is designed to manage all the aspects of a hospital's operation, such as medical, administrative, financial, and legal issues and the corresponding processing of services. With the help of PMIS, healthcare professionals can monitor patient information such as registration details, appointment scheduling, doctor's notes, billing information, and much more. It is designed to provide accurate, up-to-date, and complete information about patients at the point of care. It helps in ensuring the healthcare providers make better decisions, improve patient care, and also reduce the chances of errors and omissions. Example: A hospital uses PMIS to streamline patient registration, appointment scheduling, and billing processes. The system allows the hospital to keep track of all patient interactions, from admission to discharge, and even follow-up appointments. A clinic uses PMIS to manage patient records. The system allows the clinic to store patient medical history, treatment plans, and test results in one place, making it easier for doctors to access and review the information.
