Health Dataset Catalogue Catalogue ()
Definition: Health Dataset Catalogue Catalogue is a grouping that covers a variety of data set catalogues related to the healthcare sector. It includes catalogues such as the Prescription Catalogue, Healthcare Facilities Catalogue, and Patient Summary Index. These catalogues contain indexed inventories of data sets where prescription data are stored, a list of all accredited healthcare facilities, and an indexed inventory of all patient summaries respectively. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Dataset Catalogue Catalogue is a comprehensive collection of healthcare-related data set catalogues. The Prescription Catalogue mitigates fragmentation through disperse data sets by providing an indexed inventory of the data sets where prescription data are stored. The Healthcare Facilities Catalogue provides a list of all accredited healthcare facilities, ensuring that users have access to reliable and verified information. The Patient Summary Index provides an indexed inventory of all patient summaries in a de-centralised architecture, allowing for efficient access and retrieval of patient information. Example: For instance, a healthcare provider could use the Prescription Catalogue to locate specific prescription data across various data sets, reducing the time and effort required to find this information. Similarly, a patient could use the Healthcare Facilities Catalogue to find accredited healthcare facilities in their area. The Patient Summary Index could be used by healthcare professionals to quickly access a patient's medical history.
