Heatlh Data Space Enablers ()
Definition: Heatlh Data Space Enablers ABB is a Grouping that covers a range of elements that facilitate the creation, management, and utilization of health data spaces. These elements are designed to support the application services within the health data space, enabling efficient data exchange, storage, and analysis. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Data Space Enablers ABB is a comprehensive catalogue of elements that are integral to the functioning of health data spaces. These elements are designed to provide application services, such as the EHDS2 Node, which is a critical component in the health data space. The EHDS2 Node Application Service, for instance, is designed to facilitate the exchange, storage, and analysis of health data within the data space. These enablers are crucial in ensuring the efficient operation of health data spaces, allowing for the seamless exchange and analysis of health data. Example: An example of a Health Data Space Enabler is the EHDS2 Node. This is an application service that is designed to facilitate the exchange, storage, and analysis of health data within the data space. It is a critical component in the health data space, ensuring the efficient operation of the data space and allowing for the seamless exchange and analysis of health data.
