Health Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NBI) ()
Definition: Health Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NBI) ABB is a grouping that covers the legal and regulatory frameworks that govern the exchange, access, and use of health-related data and information across different health systems, platforms, and applications. It includes laws, regulations, and policies that ensure the standardization, compatibility, and interoperability of health information systems and technologies. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NBI) ABB is crucial in the modern healthcare sector, where the seamless exchange of health information is vital for improving patient care, enhancing health outcomes, and facilitating health research. It encompasses a wide range of elements, including data standards, health information exchange protocols, privacy and security regulations, and other legal requirements related to health information technology. The NBI ABB ensures that health information systems and technologies can communicate, exchange data, and use the information that has been exchanged effectively, thereby promoting interoperability in the health sector. Example: Examples of application include the implementation of electronic health records (EHRs) systems that comply with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and other relevant laws and regulations. Another example is the development of health information exchange (HIE) platforms that adhere to the standards set by the Office of the National Coordinator for Health Information Technology (ONC).
