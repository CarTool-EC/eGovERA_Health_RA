Health Data Policy Catalogue ()
Definition: Health Data Policy Catalogue ABB is a Grouping that covers a range of policies related to the management, processing, and use of health data. These policies provide guidelines for Member States on various aspects of health data use, including the secondary use of health data, the definition of databases related to diagnosis, and the definition of databases related to patient summary. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Health Data Policy Catalogue ABB includes three main elements: 'Code of conduct for secondary use of health data', 'Data policy on diagnosis', and 'Data policy on patient summary'. The 'Code of conduct for secondary use of health data' is a policy that outlines the code of conduct for Member States in managing and processing genetic, biometric, or health data for research or statistical purposes. The 'Data policy on diagnosis' provides rules and guidelines for the definition of databases related to Diagnosis. The 'Data policy on patient summary' outlines the rules and guidelines for the definition of databases related to Patient Summary. Each of these elements plays a crucial role in ensuring the ethical and efficient use of health data. Example: For instance, a Member State may use this policy to guide the use of genetic data in a research study on a specific genetic disorder. The policy would provide guidelines on how to ethically and efficiently manage and process this data. As an example, a Member State could use this policy to define a database for storing and managing data related to the diagnosis of various diseases. The policy would provide guidelines on how to structure this database and what data to include. For example, a Member State could use this policy to define a database for storing and managing patient summary data. The policy would provide guidelines on what data to include in the patient summary and how to structure this database.
