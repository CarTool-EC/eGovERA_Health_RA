Health Medical Imaging Workflow & Data Exchange Technical Interoperability Agreement Solutions ()
Definition: Health Medical Imaging Workflow & Data Exchange Technical Interoperability Agreement Solutions ABB is a Grouping that covers a range of technical solutions designed to facilitate the workflow and data exchange in the field of health medical imaging. These solutions are based on interoperability agreements, which are contracts that define the technical standards and protocols for data exchange between different healthcare systems. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The elements contained in this grouping are all related to the workflow and data exchange in radiology and medical imaging. They include the IHE SWF: Radiology Scheduled Workflow, which is a contract that outlines the workflow for scheduled radiology procedures; the IHE XDS-I.b: Cross-Enterprise Document, which is a contract that defines the standards for document exchange across different healthcare enterprises; the IHE SWF.b: Radiology Scheduled Workflow, which is a similar contract to the IHE SWF but with different specifications; and the DICOM - Medical Image Exchange Contract, which is a contract that outlines the standards for the exchange of medical images using the DICOM (Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine) standard. Example: An example of application of these elements could be a hospital that uses the IHE SWF: Radiology Scheduled Workflow to manage its radiology procedures, the IHE XDS-I.b: Cross-Enterprise Document to exchange documents with other healthcare providers, the IHE SWF.b: Radiology Scheduled Workflow for specific radiology procedures, and the DICOM - Medical Image Exchange Contract to exchange medical images with other healthcare providers.
