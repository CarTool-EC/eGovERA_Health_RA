TVI-Containers Infrastructure Enablers [Motivation] ()
Narrative: The Orchestration Infrastructure Enablers view provides insights into the relationship between the infrastructure enablers for orchestration and the orchestration enablers at the application level. This view highlights the importance of a robust and efficient infrastructure to support the seamless coordination and automation of business processes. At the infrastructure layer, several elements are involved in enabling orchestration. The Management Infrastructure Enablers play a vital role in providing the necessary tools and capabilities for managing and monitoring the orchestration processes. These enablers include components such as logger technology services, audit manager technology services, telemetry technology services, and management console technology services. They enable organizations to effectively manage and control the execution of orchestrated workflows. Additionally, the Workflow Orchestration Infrastructure Enablers provide the foundational components for modelling, executing, and monitoring complex business workflows. These enablers encompass workflow engines, service buses, event managers, and process managers. They are designed to support the seamless execution and coordination of business processes across different systems and services. Furthermore, the Containers Infrastructure Enablers play a crucial role in providing the necessary runtime environment for orchestrating and deploying containerized applications. These enablers include container orchestrator technology services, container manager technology services, container registry technology services, and containers themselves. They enable organizations to encapsulate and manage their applications in lightweight, isolated containers, facilitating easy deployment and scalability. The orchestration enablers at the application level work in collaboration with the infrastructure enablers to facilitate the seamless automation of business processes. These enablers include components and services that support activities such as process modelling, integration, event handling, and service orchestration. They empower organizations to define and automate their business workflows, integrating disparate systems and services into cohesive end-to-end processes. The synergy between the orchestration infrastructure enablers and the orchestration enablers at the application level is essential for the success of process automation initiatives. The infrastructure enablers provide the necessary runtime environment, management capabilities, and containerization support to execute orchestrated processes, while the orchestration enablers empower organizations to design and configure complex business workflows. By establishing a strong connection between the orchestration infrastructure enablers and the orchestration enablers at the application level, organizations can achieve streamlined and efficient business operations. This enables organizations to automate repetitive tasks, improve process visibility and control, and enhance overall operational efficiency.
