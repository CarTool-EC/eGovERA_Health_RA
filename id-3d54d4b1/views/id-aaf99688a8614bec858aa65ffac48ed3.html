SV-Data Mapping Catalogue [Motivation] ()
NARRATIVE: The SV-Data Mapping Catalogue [Motivation] view extends Semantic view and provides a comprehensive framework for leveraging the business agnostic business information base and the data mapping catalogue. The first element in this view is the 'Business Agnostic Business Information Base', which is a Diagram Model-group ABB. This base is a general-purpose business information base that is not tied to any specific business context or domain. It contains two sub-elements: 'Availability' and 'Digital Asset', both of which are Business Object ABBs. 'Availability' is a business object that represents the availability information. This could include data about the availability of resources, services, or any other business elements. The 'Digital Asset' business object represents the digital asset information. This could include data about digital resources such as software, digital media, or any other digital assets. The second main element in this view is the 'Data Mapping Catalogue', which is a Data Object ABB. This catalogue is a data object that aims to index an inventory of rules or procedures on how data from different sources and domains can be linked and integrated. This could include mapping rules, transformation rules, integration procedures, or any other relevant information. The relationship between these elements is that the 'Data Mapping Catalogue' uses the data from the 'Business Agnostic Business Information Base' to create a comprehensive and integrated view of the business data. This allows for a more effective and efficient use of the data, as it can be easily linked and integrated across different sources and domains. The motivation behind this view is to provide a comprehensive framework for leveraging business data. By using a business agnostic business information base and a data mapping catalogue, businesses can effectively manage their data and use it to drive business decisions and actions. This can lead to improved business performance and competitiveness.
